## Design Context

### Users
Primary viewers are hiring managers, collaborators, and clients reviewing Kim Jaegwan's portfolio.
They need to understand identity, design sensibility, and problem-solving orientation quickly from the landing view.

### Brand Personality
Editorial, premium, restrained.
The tone should feel confident, calm, and crafted rather than loud or decorative.

### Aesthetic Direction
Use a dark, minimal hero with oversized typography, subtle motion, and a sculptural line field inspired by portfolio showcase sites.
The reference mood is cinematic and precise, but the result should remain distinct in wording and implementation.

### Design Principles
1. Identity must read immediately.
2. Motion should support atmosphere, not slow comprehension.
3. Typography carries most of the visual weight.
4. The hero should feel premium without adding unnecessary UI chrome.
5. Every later section should remain secondary to selected work and case-study clarity.
